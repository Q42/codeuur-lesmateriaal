Programming for Kids - by CodeUur

For this lesson we’re going to use Scratch. You can download it on your computer, or run it in an internet browser.

Open Scratch on your computer, or surf to http://scratch.mit.edu and click on the “try it” (“probeer het” in Dutch) button.

Tip: Use the world icon on the top left to change the language.

When you’re done with your project, you can download it to the computer and mail it to yourself so you can continue on it later.

Have fun! If you have questions afterwards, get in contact with les@codeuur.nl. 

Lesson: A Maze - Save the princess!

A princess is in trouble! We’re going to make a maze game, where the cat (our Hero 😃) has to go through the maze and save the princess.

Good luck!

1. Make the cat smaller

This is the start screen. At the top right, select the “Shrink” option, and click on the cat. Keep clicking on the cat until it’s small enough to fit in the maze you’re going to make!

Next, drag the cat to the bottom right, the start position of the maze.

2. Draw the maze

At the bottom left, select “Stage”. In the right panel, select the tab “Backdrops” and then you’ll see the background canvas to draw on. Select a color and a brush size, and make your maze!

3. Teach the cat to move - programming!

5. Hitting a wall

When the cat hits a wall, he should say “Au!!” and move back to the starting point.

6. Reaching the princess

Add the princess by clicking on the “choose sprite from library” button. Now search for the princess, or choose a different character!

A character, or something that can be moved, is called a “Sprite”.

Now, when we reach the princess, win!
